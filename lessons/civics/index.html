About this curriculum

This is an SEL informed civics curriculum with a focus on community.

Kids learn why we have rules, rights and responsibilities — and how their actions can impact their communities. They will discover what it means to be a good citizen and create their own "classroom constitution": a list of rules framed as promises to each other, to help protect the rights of everyone in the class.

The core running metaphor throughout the curriculum is the idea that every community has a heart at the center, and we are the puzzle pieces that make up that heart. When our pieces fit together, the heart is strong. If they don't — because we're not following rules, fulfilling responsibilities, or hurting each other — the heart gets weak. But working together, good deeds, and apologies can always fix the heart.

Each unit, students add what they learned to their own puzzle piece, which they use at the end of the curriculum to sign their classroom constitution — creating their own heart with a piece from each student.

This curriculum meets Social Sciences standards for Illinois and Chicago Public Schools, SY 2025–2026.
