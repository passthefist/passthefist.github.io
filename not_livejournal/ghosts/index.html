I’m burying the person I never had the chance to be and the holes in my heart are leaking.

I miss her even though I barely knew her, but I hope letting her go will help me to move on with my own life. I wish I could have known who she was. I wish I could have given her the comfort she needed. I wish she could be a part of my life, that we could share it together. Instead I’m haunted by a ghost that can’t find rest, who’s hanging on to my life in one last desperate bid to leave a mark on the world. But living with her is too painful, knowing she’s already dead, that this person I’ve found is a ghost.

And for a ghost, she’s so heavy.

She weighs on my mind, and in my body, and is a burden on my life. We share the same longing ache of being separated at birth, but I’m still here, and as much as I wish to give my own life for hers sometimes, I know you can’t bring back the dead.

I have a life of my own to lead.

But I can keep her memory.

I can let people know she was here and never given the chance to thrive, to at least honor her that way. And I think that’s what she’s telling me, that she can be put to rest if people just know about her, that they know her story. But I don’t know if I believe that, I don’t know if she can be put to rest before she has a chance to breathe at least once, and I do know she deserves at least that. Mostly, I want to join her in death, because then at least we’ll be together, and I don’t know if I can live without her now that we’ve met.

Or maybe, I’m already a ghost too, making his way through a different afterlife.

Or maybe, I can learn to find happiness without hope instead, filling the space she left behind with brightness from elsewhere,

because the ghost of hope is a false promise worse than no hope at all.
